ABOUT ME

Since my early years in primary school, I have always been a very quiet, calm, and serious student. I was very studious: I often spent my breaks revising my lessons or doing math exercises. I enjoyed studying and understanding, and was curious to learn new things. I was especially passionate about mathematics. Thanks to this attitude, I was always at the top of the class in every subject except for physical education. That area was more challenging for me. I was not very comfortable doing some sports activities, especially swimming. Although it was not my strength, I never gave up and always participated with full commitment.

In secondary school, I always chose the science and math track. I liked challenges and wanted to keep learning more complex things. The first two years went well; I delivered excellent results and was well organised. But in the third year, I went through a difficult time: I had to change schools in the middle of the year. The new school had already covered a lot of material I had not seen yet, and I felt a bit lost. I had to work even harder to catch up. It was stressful and took a lot of time, but I was determined to succeed. To achieve my goal, I learned to manage my time more effectively, and, step by step, I began to improve. I consider that this challenge contributed to my personal development. It taught me important values: I learned to be patient and organised, as results do not come immediately.

After secondary school, I decided to go into industrial IT studies. I knew almost nothing about computing. In secondary school, my only IT course was about the basics of Word, so I had no knowledge of programming, electronics, or embedded systems. But this field had intrigued me for a long time. I was interested in web applications, connected objects, and websites. In the first year, I quickly realised how different university was from secondary school. There were many more demands and more subjects to study, and the level was much higher. I wasn’t prepared for this change. At the beginning, it was very hard for me. It was a completely new world. There were a lot of technical concepts, programming languages, electronics... these were subjects I was discovering for the first time in my life. I often felt pessimistic because I thought I was not good enough. But I did not give up. I attended tutoring sessions, completed additional exercises, and, above all, took the time to truly understand what I was doing. Thanks to these efforts, I improved in areas that initially seemed impossible, such as the analysis course.

I have not yet finished my studies, but I am proud of how far I have come. These years have helped me build confidence in myself and my abilities. I also learned what it means to have discipline, because motivation alone is insufficient. Outside my studies, I enjoy walking and admiring nature, especially flowers. These moments help me release stress, clear my mind, and improve my concentration.
